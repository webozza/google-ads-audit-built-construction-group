Section 10

Recommendations

Why the ads aren’t generating leads (what’s broken)

The account is getting impressions, but lead volume is being blocked by three core issues:

1) Low click-through rate (CTR): Overall CTR is 0.42%, so the ads are not earning enough qualified clicks to generate enquiries consistently.

2) Algorithm can’t learn: Campaigns are set to Maximise Conversions while conversion volume is extremely low (only 1 recorded conversion). This limits optimisation and wastes spend.

3) Tracking signal is incomplete: ‘Submit lead form’ and ‘Contact’ tags are inactive, meaning real leads may not be measured correctly — and Google cannot optimise toward them.

What we will do to increase performance and get leads

Priority A — Fix tracking + lead signals (Week 1):
Reactivate form + contact conversions, verify GA4/GTM events, and add call tracking so every enquiry is measured properly.

Priority B — Improve relevance to lift CTR (Week 1–2):
Tighten keywords (more Phrase/Exact for high intent), build a strong negative keyword list from search terms, and rewrite RSAs with Wellington + service intent + stronger CTAs.

Priority C — Adjust bidding for low-conversion accounts (Week 2–3):
Temporarily move from Maximise Conversions to a traffic-learning phase (e.g., Maximise Clicks with a CPC cap) until conversions are consistent, then shift back to conversion-based bidding with a clear target.

Priority D — Landing page optimisation (Week 3–4):
Simplify the enquiry path: stronger above-the-fold offer, fewer form fields, mobile click-to-call, and added trust proof (local projects, testimonials, licences/insurance).

30-day action plan (clear steps)

Days 1–3: Fix inactive conversion tags + verify tracking + call conversions.

Days 4–10: Search term clean-up, negatives, tighter match types, new ad copy.

Days 11–20: Bidding adjustment + budget reallocation to highest-intent areas.

Days 21–30: Landing page CRO improvements + ongoing testing.
